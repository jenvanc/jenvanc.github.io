Cookie Chemistry

Cookies are a perfect way of seeing the chemistry of baking at work. Just take a look.

Tessa Arias

You can clearly see the impact of changing the ingredients in a simple chocolate chip cookie recipe. What is going on inside of that cookie though? What is is about changing these things that makes such a dramatic difference? First, we have to look at what each of our key ingredients are doing for the cookie.

Ingredient Effect
Butter Butter is made of fats, water, and dairy solids. When the butter begins to melt, it begins to break apart into its key components. The water is released and makes steam, this is part of what makes your cookies light and fluffy.
Eggs Eggs are the binding agent in your cookie, they are what makes your dough change from pliable to more structred and held together. Without the proteins in the eggs, your dough would not hold its shape.
Sugar Sugar is a key factor in your cookies. The ratio of white to brown sugar, the temperature your cookie is baked at and how the suger is incorporated into the batter make a huge difference in the end result.
Flour Flour is important because it creates gluten when exposed to moisture. Gluten is a kind of protein that helps trap gases inside the cookie to create rise. This is not the only thing that gives rise to your cookie and this is why you can sometimes substitue plain all-purpose flour for alternative flours like rye flour.s
